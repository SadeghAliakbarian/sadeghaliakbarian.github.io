Sadegh Aliakbarian

I'm a PhD student at the Australian National University, working under supervision of Dr. Lars Petersson (ANU/CSIRO), Dr. Mathieu Salzmann (EPFL), and Dr. Basura Fernando (A-STAR) and have pieces of advice from Prof. Stephen Gould (ANU/ACRV). I'm now an applied scientist intern at Amazon Science. Prior to that, I spent time at Facebook Reality Labs, FiveAI, and Qualcomm AI Research as a research intern working on different aspects of machine learning and computer vision.

My research interests are machine learning and computer vision, with the focus on generative models and human motion/activity understanding.

Research

I'm interested in computer vision and machine learning. Much of my research is about generative modeling of sequences, especially for the tasks that are stochastic in nature, e.g., diverse human motion prediction. I'm also interested in adversarial machine learning, with the focus on adversarial defense and adaptive attacks.
